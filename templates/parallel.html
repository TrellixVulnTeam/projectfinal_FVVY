How to recognize parallel circuit

When two or more resistors are individually connected between the same two points, they are in parallel with each other.
A parallel circuit provides more than one path for current called branch.

  • When resitors are connected in parallel, the total resistance of the circuit decreases. The total resistance of a parallel circuit is always less than the value of the smallest resistor in that circuit. $$R_t = {1 \over { {1\over R_1} + {1\over R_2} + {1\over R_3} + ... + {1\over R_n}}} $$
  • The reciprocal of resistance is conductance, symbolized by G. it is measure of the ease with with which current is established. Formula : $$ G = {1 \over R }$$
